# Get Involved with YESDR


## Before You Get Started

Before contributing, we recommend reviewing the following pages:

- [**Consortium**](../consortium/overview.md) - Details YESDR Consortium organizational structure
- [**Membership**](../consortium/membership.md) – Details on membership categories, eligibility, and participation models  
- [**Policies**](../consortium/policies.md) – Governance, contribution rules, code of conduct, and IP guidelines  
- [**Current Status**](../currentstatus/current_status.md) – Ongoing work, completed milestones, and planned developments  


---

## Ways to Get Involved

### Research & Technical Contributions
Members and contributors may participate in:

- Development of PHY, MAC, and higher-layer radio modules
- Development of core network modules
- Development of protocols
- AI/ML/DL-based radio intelligence, including:
  - Spectrum sensing  
  - Modulation classification  
  - Channel prediction  
  - Channel ON–OFF duration prediction  
- Proposing extensions aligned with the YESDR standard architecture  

Active research areas are listed and updated in [Current Status](../currentstatus/current_status.md).

---

### Software Contributions
Contributors can:

- Develop Python or C++ modules aligned with YESDR APIs  
- Improve performance, modularity, and portability  
- Submit bug reports, fixes, and feature enhancements  

All software contributions must comply with the YESDR policies.

---

### Documentation & Knowledge Sharing
You may also contribute by:

- Improving technical documentation and specifications  
- Writing tutorials, experiment workflows, and lab guides  
- Assisting with API standardization  
- Supporting educational adoption across institutions  

Documentation contributions follow the same review and policy process.

---

### Academic Collaboration
Institutions and research groups can:

- Adopt YESDR for SDR, 5G, and 6G laboratory courses  
- Use YESDR for undergraduate, postgraduate, and doctoral research  
- Collaborate on joint publications and technical reports  
- Deploy YESDR-based academic or research testbeds  

---

### Industry & Startup Engagement
Industry partners may:

- Prototype and evaluate wireless products  
- Use YESDR as a reference implementation  
- Collaborate on applied research and pilots  
- Sponsor features, testbeds, or student innovation  


---

## Contribution Workflow

1. Review [Membership](../consortium/membership.md), [Policies](../consortium/policies.md), and [Current Status](../currentstatus/current_status.md)
2. Identify the working group
3. Identify an active or proposed contribution area  
4. Follow the defined contribution guidelines  
5. Submit your contribution for technical and policy review
